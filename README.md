# 🛡️ OS Guardian (開源守護者)

Welcome to **OS Guardian**, a gamified web application tailored for tech communities and open-source clubs. 

Traditional tech clubs often face challenges with member engagement, low attendance at workshops, and a lack of daily interactions. OS Guardian solves this by transforming community participation into a fun, rewarding, and RPG-like experience. By turning everyday club activities into engaging quests, we help communities build a stronger sense of belonging.

## ✨ Core Features

* **🐾 Virtual Pet Companions (護法寵物養成)**
Adopt and raise a unique tech-themed guardian—whether it's a Software Cat, an Open-Source Dog, or a Terminal Mecha. Your pet's growth and mood are directly linked to your club participation. Interact with them daily to keep their "battery" or "satiety" full!

* **💻 Bounty Task Board (賞金任務系統)**
We turned boring club duties into exciting quests. Members can earn "OS Coins" by completing specific actions, such as attending study groups, answering technical questions in group chats, helping clean the club room, or successfully submitting an open-source PR.

* **⚖️ Dual-Track Economy (雙軌決策商城)**
Make strategic choices with your hard-earned coins. You can choose the "Entertainment Route" by purchasing virtual items (like laser pointers or battery upgrades) to level up your pet. Alternatively, take the "Practical Route" and save your coins to redeem real-world physical rewards at the end of the semester.

* **💬 Social Feed (社群動態分享)**
A dedicated micro-blogging space for members to share daily coding struggles, debugging victories, or just show off their fully leveled-up pets. It creates a warm and active environment for everyone to connect.

* **🔮 Daily Geek Fortune (每日通靈占卜)**
Start your coding session with a randomized fortune draw! Get your daily "Geek Fortune" to find out your lucky tech stack, debugging success rate, and potential compiler warnings for the day.

## 🎯 Project Goal
This project was initially prototyped during a "Vibe Coding" session to demonstrate how AI tools can bridge the gap between creative ideas and practical community management. We hope OS Guardian makes contributing to open-source not just a task, but a joyful journey.
